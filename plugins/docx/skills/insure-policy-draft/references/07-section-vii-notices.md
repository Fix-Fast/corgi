SECTION VII: NOTICES AND CONDITIONS

1) Change in Control: The Named Insured must provide us with written notice as soon as practicable, but no later than thirty (30) days after any Change in Control. Such notice must include the date and nature of the transaction and any information we reasonably request for underwriting and evaluation. Upon a Change in Control, coverage under this Policy for Bodily Injury, Property Damage, or Personal and Advertising Injury that occurs or is committed on or after the effective date of the Change in Control will continue only with our prior written consent, which may be conditioned upon additional premium, revised limits, retentions, exclusions, or other terms we require. Absent our written consent, this Policy applies only to Occurrences or offenses that take place before the effective date of the Change in Control.

2) Bankruptcy: Bankruptcy or insolvency of any Insured shall not relieve us of our obligations under this Policy, provided the Insured shall provide any reasonable assistance and cooperation necessary to lift any automatic stay or injunction that may apply to the payment of any proceeds of this Policy. In the event a liquidation or reorganization proceeding is commenced by or against the Named Insured (under federal bankruptcy law or any similar law), the Named Insured and the Insureds agree to waive and release any automatic stay or injunction which may apply in such proceeding to this Policy or any proceeds of this Policy.

3) Notices Provision: Once a Demand is made against an Insured, it is an express condition precedent to any of your rights under this Policy that you give us immediate written notice thereof and provide us with copies of all documentation comprising the Demand as well as all authorizations, cooperation and assistance we may thereafter request. In no event may written notice to us be made more than 30 calendar days after actual or constructive receipt of a Demand by the Insured. Strict adherence to this paragraph is an express condition precedent to any of your rights under this Policy, and it shall be presumed that we are materially prejudiced by any deviation from your obligations under this paragraph.

4) Cooperation:

    a) As a condition precedent to coverage, each Insured must cooperate with us in the investigation, defense and settlement of any Demand and in enforcing any right of contribution or indemnity against any person or entity who may be liable to an Insured for the loss. Any failure of any Insured to cooperate that prejudices our ability to investigate or defend any Demand shall nullify coverage for such Demand.

    b) Upon an Occurrence, or receipt of a Demand, for which coverage is sought under this Policy, as an express condition precedent to coverage under this Policy, no Insured shall, without our prior written consent, communicate orally or in writing with the claimant or the claimant’s representative or attorney concerning the circumstances of the Demand or Occurrence. If such communication is mandated by law, we shall be notified in advance of any such communication in writing, and we shall not unreasonably withhold written consent to such communication. Any Insured’s violation of this provision, even without prejudice to us, shall act as a forfeiture of any coverage that may have otherwise existed for such Demand or Occurrence.

    c) Interrelated Occurrences: Interrelated Occurrences shall be deemed to constitute one Occurrence, and shall be deemed to have first occurred on the date of the earliest Occurrence comprising Interrelated Occurrences, regardless of whether such date is before or during the Policy Period. All Demands based upon or arising out of the same Occurrence or Interrelated Occurrences shall be considered a single Demand.

    d) Other Insurance: If other valid and collectible insurance is available to the Insured for a Loss we cover under Coverages A, B, or C of this Policy (whether such insurance is stated to be primary, contributing, excess or contingent), our obligations are limited as follows:

        i) Primary Insurance: This insurance is primary, except when paragraph 2 below applies. If this insurance is primary, and the Insured has other insurance which is also primary, we will share with that other insurance on a pro-rata basis.

        ii) Excess Insurance: This insurance shall be excess over (i) any Fire, Extended Coverage, Builder’s Risk, Installation Floater or similar property insurance covering the Insured’s work or property; (ii) any fire, lightning or explosion insurance covering premises rented to the Insured or temporarily occupied by the Insured; (iii) any insurance purchased by the Insured to cover its liability as a tenant for Property Damage to premises rented or temporarily occupied by the Insured; (iv) any other primary insurance available to the Insured covering liability for damages arising out of premises or operations for which the Insured has been added as an additional insured on another policy; and (v) any valid and collectible primary insurance covering liability arising out of the maintenance or use of aircraft, Autos or watercraft, to the extent not subject to the Aircraft/Auto/Watercraft exclusion under Coverage A of this Policy. When this insurance is excess, we will have no duty to defend the Insured against any Demand if any other insurer has a duty to defend the Insured against that Demand. This Policy is not intended to share in a defense or otherwise allocate exposure where another policy is providing coverage to the Insured for a Demand.

    e) Premium Audit: If this Policy is subject to audit (as indicated in the Declarations or by endorsement), the premium shown as auditable is a deposit premium only. We will compute the final premium for each audit period based on our rules and rates and the actual exposures (such as sales or payroll) that occurred during that period. If the earned premium is more than the deposit premium paid, the Named Insured will be responsible for the difference (subject to any minimum premium); if less, we will return the difference to the Named Insured. The Named Insured must keep records of the information we need for premium calculation and send us copies of such records upon request.

    f) Representations By Insured: By accepting this Policy, the Named Insured agrees that: (a) the statements in the Declarations, Application and any supplemental materials are true, accurate and complete; (b) those statements are based upon representations made to us by the Insured; and (c) we have issued this Policy in reliance upon the Insured’s

representations. This Policy is void in any case of material misrepresentation, concealment or fraud by the Insured as it relates to this insurance.

    g) Action Against Us: No action shall lie against us unless, as an express condition precedent thereto, there has been full compliance with all terms, conditions, and covenants of this Policy. No person or organization shall have any right under this Policy to join us as a party to any action against Insureds to determine the Insured’s liability, nor shall we be impleaded by the Insureds or their legal representatives. The Insured’s liability for Loss must first be fixed by a final, non-appealable judgment or by a written settlement signed by the Insured, the claimant, and us before any action may be brought to recover under this Policy.

    h) Mandatory Bilateral Binding Arbitration: We and the Insureds agree that any and all disputes, controversies, or claims arising out of or relating to this Policy or its breach, termination, interpretation, or invalidity, shall first be negotiated in a non-binding mediation. Sixty calendar days following the declaration of a mediation impasse, the dispute may be submitted to final and binding arbitration pursuant to such rules and procedures as the parties may agree. If the parties cannot agree, the arbitration shall be administered by JAMS in accordance with its then prevailing Streamlined Arbitration Rules. In the event of arbitration, the panel shall consist of one arbitrator selected by JAMS, and the arbitrator shall not have the right to award punitive, exemplary, bad faith, or extra-contractual damages. In any arbitration, each party will bear its own legal fees and expenses. Judgment on any award rendered by the arbitrator may be entered in any court having jurisdiction thereof. We and the Insureds agree to waive their right to have any dispute adjudicated in a court of law and agree that final binding arbitration is the sole adjudicatory remedy of any disputes, controversies, or claims arising out of or relating to the Policy. We and the Insureds agree that any mediation or arbitration will take place in the state indicated in Item 1 of the Declarations, and will be governed by the substantive laws of the state indicated in Item 1 of the Declarations.We and all Insureds knowingly agree that this provision eliminates the ability to have such disputes adjudicated in a public state or federal court. We and all Insureds further agree that, in the event a motion to compel arbitration is necessitated by one party’s refusal to adhere to this provision, and if such a motion to compel arbitration is successful, the unsuccessful party shall reimburse and indemnify the successful party for the fees and costs associated with seeking the enforcement of this section.This Section only applies to the extent not prohibited by the law pursuant to which this Policy is interpreted.

    i) Named Insured Provisions: The Named Insured specified in the Declarations is authorized to act on behalf of all Insureds with respect to giving and receiving notice of cancellation or nonrenewal, paying premiums and receiving any return premiums, and accepting endorsements to this Policy.

    j) Transfer of Rights of Recovery (Subrogation): If the Insured has rights to recover all or part of any payment we have made under this Policy, those rights are transferred to us. The Insured must do nothing after a loss to impair those rights and must help us execute any papers and take any reasonable action necessary to secure our rights. If we recover any amount after paying Loss under this Policy, such recovery shall belong to us and shall not reinstate, replenish, or otherwise increase any Limit of Liability under this Policy. Recoveries shall be applied first to our reasonable recovery expenses and then to amounts paid by us.

    k) Changes: Notice to any agent or knowledge possessed by any agent or other person acting on our behalf shall not effect a waiver or change in any part of this Policy or stop us from asserting any right under the terms of this Policy. The provisions of this Policy shall not be waived, changed or modified, except by a written endorsement issued to form a part of this Policy.

    l) Cancellation and Nonrenewal

        i) Cancellation

            (1) We may cancel this Policy:

                (a) For nonpayment of premium by mailing or delivering written notice of cancellation to the first Named Insured at the mailing address shown in the Declarations at least 10 days before the effective date of cancellation.

                (b) For any other reason permitted by applicable law by mailing or delivering written notice of cancellation to the first Named Insured at least 30 days before the effective date of cancellation.

The mailing of notice shall be sufficient proof of notice. Notice to the first Named Insured shall be notice to all Insureds.

If we cancel, earned premium shall be computed pro rata unless a different calculation is required by law. If you cancel, earned

premium shall be computed at the short-rate basis unless a different calculation is required by law.

            (2) You may cancel this Policy at any time by mailing written notice to us stating when, thereafter, such cancellation shall be effective. Proof of mailing of such notice shall be sufficient proof of notice. Delivery of such written notice shall be equivalent to mailing. The effective date and hour of cancellation as stated in the notice shall become the end of the Policy Period.

If you cancel this Policy, we will return 90% of the unearned pro rata per diem premium to the Named Insured subject to a minimum retained earned premium of twenty-five percent of the total premium.

        ii) NonrenewalIf we elect not to renew this Policy, we will mail or deliver written

notice of nonrenewal to the Named Insured at least 30 days before the expiration date, or any longer period required by law. The mailing of notice shall be sufficient proof of notice.

    m) Assessability: This Policy is non-assessable.

    n) Titles of Paragraphs: The titles or headings of sections, provisions, or paragraphs of this Policy are intended for reference only and are not to be considered in interpreting this insurance contract.

    o) Coordination of Insuring Agreement: In the event that any Demand is the subject of coverage under more than one Insuring Agreement, the largest applicable Limit of Liability, and its corresponding Retention, shall be applied to the entire Demand. Under no circumstances will more than one Limit of Liability or more than one Insuring Agreement be available to any Demand.

    p) Non-Stacking of Limits: If this Policy and any other insurance policy, which we or any affiliated or related company issued to any Insured, applies to the same Demand or Occurrence:

        i) the Limit of Liability of this Policy shall be reduced to equal the same proportion of the Limit of Liability shown in the Declarations that the Limit of Liability shown in the Declarations under this Policy bears to the total limits of liability of all such policies, and;

        ii) the maximum aggregate amount payable under all such policies shall not exceed the limit of liability of the single policy which reflects the largest limit of liability.

    q) Nothing contained in this Section shall be construed to increase the Limit of Liability available under this Policy.

    r) This section does not apply to any policy issued by us or any affiliated company specifically identified as excess insurance over this Policy.
