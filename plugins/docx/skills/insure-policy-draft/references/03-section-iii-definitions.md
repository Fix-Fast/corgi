SECTION III: DEFINITIONS

For purposes of this Policy, the following terms have the meanings set forth below. Any word shown in bold in this Policy is a defined term. Where a term is defined both in this Definitions section and elsewhere in this Policy (including in any Coverage Section or endorsement), the definition stated in this Definitions section shall control.

1) Advertisement means a written or oral publication about your goods, products, or services that is published or broadcast to the public or a target market for the purpose of attracting customers or supporters. Material appearing on the Internet or similar electronic media qualifies. Only those portions of a website devoted to such promotion are an Advertisement.

2) Application means all applications, including attachments and submitted materials, for this Policy or for any policy of which this Policy is a renewal or replacement. All such applications, attachments and materials are deemed attached to and incorporated into this Policy even if not physically attached.

3) Auto means a land motor vehicle, trailer, or semitrailer designed for travel on public roads, including any attached machinery or equipment, and any other land vehicle that is subject to a compulsory financial responsibility or motor vehicle insurance law in the place where it is licensed or principally garaged. Auto does not include Worksite Equipment.

4) Bodily Injury means physical harm, sickness, or disease sustained by a person, and includes death resulting from any alleged act, error, omission, or mistake by an Insured. Bodily Injury does not include emotional distress, loss of services, or loss of consortium.

5) Change in Control means:

    a) the merger, consolidation, or combination of the Named Insured into another entity such that the Named Insured is not the surviving entity;

    b)  the acquisition by any person or entity of more than 50% of the outstanding securities, voting securities, or equity interest of the Named Insured; or

    c) the sale, transfer, lease, or other disposition of all or substantially all of the assets of the Named Insured.

6) Contaminant means any solid, liquid, gaseous, biological, thermal or radioactive substance that can irritate, corrupt or harm persons, property, water, air or soil, including by-products and waste.

7) Control means either (a) direct or indirect ownership of more than 50 percent of the outstanding voting securities of an entity or (b) the contractual right to elect, appoint, or designate a majority of an entity’s directors, trustees, managers, or equivalent governing persons.

8) Coverage Territory means:

    a) the United States of America (including its territories and possessions), Puerto Rico, and Canada;

    b) international waters or airspace, but only if the Bodily Injury or Property Damage occurs in the course of travel or transportation between any places included in 1. Above;

    c) all other parts of the world, if the Bodily Injury or Property Damage arises out of goods or products made or sold by you in the territory described in 1. above, or arises out of the activities of an Insured person whose home is in the territory described in 1. above but is away for a short time on your business, or arises out of Personal and Advertising Injury offenses that take place through the Internet or similar electronic means of communication; provided that the Insured’s responsibility to pay Loss is determined in a civil action on the merits in the territory described in 1. above, or in a settlement to which we have agreed.

9) Defense Costs means reasonable and necessary legal fees and expenses incurred in the investigation and defense of a Demand. Defense Costs do not include the salaries, overhead, or other internal costs of the Insured.

10) Demand means:

    a) a written notice received by an Insured alleging that a person or entity has been damaged by an Insured and:

        i) demanding monetary damages; or

        ii)  notifying the Insured of an intention to hold an Insured responsible for an Occurrence; or

    b) the filing of a civil lawsuit or arbitration proceeding against an Insured.

11) Employee means a natural person whose labor or service is, was, or will be directed by the Named Insured, including full-time workers, part-time workers, leased workers, and volunteer workers.

12) ERISA means the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974, as amended, including its rules and regulations, and any similar law.

13) Executive means any duly elected or appointed director, officer, member of a management or supervisory board, in-house general counsel, advisory-board member, or natural-person general partner of the Named Insured.

14) Impaired Property means tangible property, other than Your Product or Your Work, that cannot be used or is less useful because:

    a) it incorporates Your Product or Your Work that is known or thought to be defective, deficient, inadequate or dangerous; or

    b) you have failed to fulfill the terms of a contract or agreement; if such property can be restored to use by the repair, replacement, adjustment, or removal of Your Product or Your Work or the work or product of any Insured.

15) Insolvency means the appointment of a receiver, conservator, liquidator, trustee, rehabilitator, or similar official to manage, supervise, or liquidate the Named Insured, or the Named Insured becoming a debtor in possession within the meaning of the United States Bankruptcy Code or similar legal status under foreign law.

16) Insured means:

    a) the Named Insured;

    b) an Employee, but solely when acting within the scope of his or her employment.

17) Interrelated Occurrences means all Occurrences that have as a common nexus any fact, circumstance, situation, event, transaction, cause or series of related facts, circumstances, situations, events, transactions, or causes. Occurrences may be Interrelated Occurrences whether or not they involve the same Demand(s), damages, patient(s), Claimant(s), Insured(s), or legal theory(ies).

18) Limit of Liability means the applicable amounts identified in Item — of the Declarations.

19) Loss means compensatory monetary amounts (including judgments, awards and settlements) that an Insured is legally obligated to pay as compensation for a covered loss. Loss does not include fines, punitive or exemplary damages, multiplied portions of any multiple damages, penalties, taxes, or matters deemed uninsurable by law, nor do they include Defense Costs.

20) Medical Payments mean first aid at the time of the accident; necessary medical, surgical, X-ray, and dental services, including prosthetics; necessary ambulance, hospital, and professional nursing services; and funeral services.

21) Named Insured means the person(s) or organization(s) designated in the Declarations as such.

22) Occurrence means an accident, including continuous or repeated exposure to substantially the same general conditions.

23) Personal and Advertising Injury means injury, including consequential Bodily Injury, arising out of one or more of the following offenses:

    a) false arrest, detention, or imprisonment;

    b) malicious prosecution or wrongful eviction;

    c) wrongful entry, or invasion of the right of private occupancy of premises a person occupies, when committed by or for the owner, landlord, or lessor;

    d) publication, in any manner, of material that slanders or libels a person or organization, or that disparages their goods, products, or services; or

    e) publication, in any manner, of material that violates a person’s right of privacy, use of another’s advertising idea in your Advertisement, or the infringement of copyright, trade dress, or slogan in your Advertisement.

24) Policy means, collectively, these Policy Terms and Conditions, the Declarations, the Insuring Agreements, the Definitions, the Limitations of Liability and Retentions, the Exclusions, the Defense and Settlement provisions, the Notices and Conditions, the Application, and all Endorsements.

25) Policy Period means the time period identified in the Declarations.

26) Pollutants means any solid, liquid, gaseous, or thermal irritant or contaminant, including smoke, vapor, soot, fumes, acids, alkalis, chemicals, and waste.

27) Products-Completed Operations Hazard includes all Bodily Injury and Property Damage occurring away from premises you own or rent and arising out of Your Product or Your Work, except:

    a) products that are still in your physical possession; or

    b) work that has not yet been completed or abandoned. Your Work will be deemed completed at the earliest of:

        i) when all of the work called for in your contract has been completed;

        ii) when all of the work to be done at a job site has been completed if your contract calls for work at more than one site; or

        iii) when that part of the work at a job site has been put to its intended use by any person or organization other than another contractor or subcontractor working on the same project. Work that is otherwise complete will be treated as completed even if it requires service, maintenance, correction, repair, or replacement; and

28) Bodily Injury or Property Damage arising out of the transportation of property, unless the injury or damage arises out of a condition in or on a vehicle not owned or operated by you and that condition was created by the loading or unloading of that vehicle by an Insured.

29) Property Damage means:

    a) physical injury to tangible property, including all resulting loss of use of that property. All such loss of use shall be deemed to occur at the time of the physical injury that caused it; or

    b) loss of use of tangible property that is not physically injured. For the purposes of this insurance, electronic data, which includes but is not limited to facts or programs stored as or on, created or used on, or transmitted to or from computer software, including systems and applications software, hard or floppy disks, CD-ROMs, tapes, drives, cells, data processing devices or any other media used with electronically controlled equipment, is not tangible property. All such loss of use shall be deemed to occur at the time of the Occurrence that caused it.

30) Regulatory Body means any federal, state, local or foreign law enforcement or governmental authority or the enforcement unit of any securities exchange or similar self-regulating body.

31) Related Demands means all Demands based upon, arising out of, or in any way involving the same Occurrence or Interrelated Occurrences, regardless of the number of claimants, Insureds, or proceedings.

32) Retention means the amount, if any, stated in the Declarations, which shall apply to both Loss and Defense Costs.

33) Retroactive Date means the date (if any) specified in the Declarations as the Retroactive Date.

34) Senior Executive means the Chief Executive Officer, Chief Financial Officer, President, Chief Operating Officer, or General Counsel (or any individual serving in an equivalent position) of the Named Insured or any person who signed the Application.

35) Subsidiary means any entity over which the Named Insured has Control, including any not-for-profit entity exclusively controlled by the Named Insured.

36) Worksite Equipment means a land vehicle, including any attached machinery or equipment that: is designed for off-road use, such as tracked or industrial vehicles, or it is maintained for use only on premises you own or rent or on ways next to those premises;

    a) Worksite Equipment:

        i) is maintained mainly to provide mobility to permanently mounted equipment or to perform a specialized task other than transporting persons or cargo; or

        ii) is otherwise a vehicle not intended for regular travel on public roads.

    b) The following are Autos and not Worksite Equipment:

        i) any land vehicle designed for or used in travel on public roads;

        ii) any self-propelled platform or device mounted on, integrated with, or operated from an automobile or truck chassis; or

        iii) any autonomous or remotely operated ground device when operated on public roads or under a motor vehicle licensing or permitting scheme.

        iv) In all events, a land vehicle subject to a compulsory or financial responsibility law or other motor vehicle insurance law in the jurisdiction where it is licensed or principally garaged will be treated as an Auto.

37) Your Product means any goods or products, other than real property, manufactured, sold, handled, distributed, or disposed of by:

    a) an Insured; and

    b) containers (other than vehicles), materials, parts or equipment furnished in connection with such goods or products. Your Product includes warranties or representations made at any time with respect to the fitness, quality, durability, performance or use of Your Product, and the providing of or failure to provide warnings or instructions in connection with it. Your Product does not include vending machines or other property rented to or located for the use of others but not sold.

38) Your Work means:

    a) work or operations performed by you or on your behalf; and

    b) materials, parts or equipment furnished in connection with such work or operations. Your Work includes warranties or representations made at any time with respect to the fitness, quality, durability, performance or use of Your Work, and the providing of or failure to provide warnings or instructions in connection with it.
